Empirical Evidence & Research Findings

A comprehensive analysis of cybersecurity telemetry conducted by ICT 12 ST. VERONICA.

Module I: Malware Distribution Trends

Our research into Malware (Malicious Software) indicates a shift from simple destructive viruses to sophisticated Polymorphic Code. Statistical data shows that Trojan-style infections account for 58% of all initial system compromises. This data underscores the importance of our findings on the "Defense in Depth" strategy, where firewalls alone are insufficient if the malware is disguised as legitimate business software.

Module II: The Economics of Ransomware

According to current 2026 telemetry, the average downtime for an organization hit by Ransomware is 22 days. Our research team analyzed the cost-benefit of payment versus recovery; the evidence suggests that organizations with Offline Immutable Backups recover 75% faster and spend 90% less on recovery than those who attempt to negotiate with threat actors like LockBit or Conti.

Module III: Social Engineering Success Rates

The "Proof of Result" in our study identifies Phishing as the primary attack vector. Data shows that even with advanced email filters, 1 in 10 employees will still click a "Spear Phishing" link if it creates a false sense of urgency. This confirms our recommendation that Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is the single most critical technical barrier to prevent identity theft following a successful social engineering attempt.

Module IV: Network Interception & DDoS

Our analysis of Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attacks proves that 80% of open public Wi-Fi networks are vulnerable to packet sniffing. The research highlights the necessity of End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) and VPN usage. Furthermore, DDoS data shows that IoT devices now make up 40% of botnet traffic, proving that home security (changing default router passwords) is now a matter of national cybersecurity importance.
